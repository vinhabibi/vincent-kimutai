01 GHG Emissions Forecasting
Live App → Streamlit →

Full climate research project on the EDGAR dataset (1970–2018). Comprehensive EDA, time series decomposition, and LSTM vs SARIMA model comparison across CO₂, CH₄ and N₂O emissions — with a React frontend, FastAPI backend, and live Streamlit dashboard.

What I Learnt
No single model won across all gases. LSTM outperformed SARIMA on N₂O due to its ability to capture long-range non-linear dependencies, while SARIMA held the edge on CO₂ and CH₄ where patterns were more seasonal and interpretable. Model selection has to be gas-specific, not universal.
Impact
Contributed the LSTM CO₂ forecasting notebook to a team climate hackathon (stee1011/climate-hackathon) via a pull request on a feature branch, then deployed the full multi-service project — React frontend on Railway, Streamlit interface on Streamlit Cloud — as a live forecasting tool.
Skills Gained
LSTM and SARIMA time series modelling, EDGAR dataset EDA and decomposition, multi-service deployment (Railway + Streamlit Cloud), collaborative Git workflow via forks and pull requests, and Vite/React frontend configuration for production hosting.

04 Coffee Sales Analysis
Dashboard → GitHub →

E-commerce coffee shop sales analysis on 3K+ transactions ($1.1M revenue) — revenue trends by product, time-of-day patterns, seasonal peaks, and AOV breakdown across a full year using Power BI and DAX.

What I Learnt
Built DAX measures using SUMMARIZE & TOPN to identify peak hour (10 AM). Learned cross-filtering — one month slicer driving all 6 visuals simultaneously. Discovered that a near-even 33/34/34% morning/afternoon/night split means demand is 24/7, not just morning-heavy as assumed.
Key Numbers
$1.1M revenue · 3K+ transactions · $31.65 avg order · Latte & Cappuccino = 45% of revenue · March peaks at 15.9K sales · July dips to 6.4K · Monday AOV highest at $31.92
Skills Gained
Power BI dashboard design, DAX calculated measures, CSV data modelling, business analytics storytelling, revenue vs transaction volume comparison, and seasonal trend identification for campaign planning.

05 Warehouse & Retail Sales Analysis
Dashboard → GitHub →

EDA and machine learning on 307,645 warehouse and retail sales transactions — uncovering product mix, supplier concentration, and seasonal demand patterns, then building an XGBoost model to predict retail sales (R² 0.9145).

What I Learnt
Discovered Retail Sales and Retail Transfers correlate at 0.96 — transfers are a near-perfect leading indicator of revenue. Solved a high-cardinality problem (385 suppliers) by switching from one-hot to target encoding, cutting ~395 columns down to 1 with zero accuracy loss.
Key Numbers
307K transactions · Wine = ~42% of volume · 385 suppliers target-encoded · December peak in both channels · XGBoost R² = 0.9145 (vs Random Forest 0.9041) · Tuned RMSE 0.2908
Skills Gained
Log transformation for skewed data, target encoding for high-cardinality categoricals, time-based feature engineering (Quarter, WeekOfYear, IsWeekend), XGBoost vs Random Forest benchmarking, and GridSearchCV hyperparameter tuning.
